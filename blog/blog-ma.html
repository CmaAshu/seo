The Core Strategy: "Easy vs. Difficult" Mix

  • The "Easy 65" Bucket: Topics like ABC, Budget, Decision Theory, and Divisional Performance cover ~65 marks. Target: 45-50 marks.
  • The "Contingency" Bucket: Complex Marginal Costing Case Studies, Standard Costing (Overheads), and pure Theory. Target: Push from Pass to Exemption.

üöÄ Phase 1: The "Confidence Booster" (Days 1‚Äì7)

Focus: Locking in the easiest and most scoring "Pairs" identified in the trend analysis.

  • Days 1-2: Activity Based Costing (ABC): Guaranteed question (Q2B). Focus on Cost Drivers & Cost Pools. Solve 5-7 sums.
  • Day 3: Theory: Intro to MA: Financial vs. Cost vs. Mgmt Accounting. Remember headings/keywords. Pairs with ABC.
  • Days 4-6: Budget & Budgetary Control: Priority on Cash Budget (tough questions trending), Production Budget, and Raw Material Budget.
  • Day 7: Buffer & Weekly Review: Revise formulas for ABC & Budget. Attempt 1 Past Paper question from each.

‚öôÔ∏è Phase 2: The "Technical Core" (Days 8‚Äì16)

Focus: Tackling the heavyweights. Strategy advises changing the learning order for Standard Costing.

  • Days 8-11: Standard Costing: Strategy Shift: Do NOT start with Material/Labor. Priority 1: Fixed & Variable Overhead Variances. Priority 2: Sales Variances. Priority 3: Material & Labor.
  • Days 12-15: Marginal Costing (Basics): BEP, Margin of Safety, PV Ratio. Warning: Master Q4 (Application) but avoid the 14-mark "Case Study" (Q3) in exams if possible.
  • Day 16: Decision Making Tools: Key Factor, Make or Buy, Export pricing.

üé∞ Phase 3: The "Jackpot" Topics (Days 17‚Äì23)

Focus: High scoring, low effort. Question 7 is often called a "Lottery Question".

  • Days 17-19: Divisional Performance: EVA (Economic Value Added), ROI, RI. Prediction: High chance of EVA questions.
  • Day 20: Learning Curve: Focus on Log/Anti-log based questions. Part of the "Lottery" Question 7.
  • Days 21-22: Decision Theory: Decision Trees, Maximin, Minimax, Probabilistic Budget.
  • Day 23: Transfer Pricing: Pairs with Responsibility Accounting. Often ignored but probability increasing. Cover basics well.

üõ°Ô∏è Phase 4: Theory & Consolidation (Days 24‚Äì27)

Focus: The safety net. Theory secures 14 Marks (Q2A & Q8B).

  • Day 24: Responsibility Accounting: Pure Theory (Investment/Profit Centers). Read the "Super 10" questions.
  • Day 25: Theory Revision: Revise all Theory concepts. Theory + MCQs = ~44 Marks (Passing Zone).
  • Days 26-27: MCQ Marathon: Source: Institute MCQ Bank. Focus on Marginal Costing and Divisional Performance MCQs.

üèÅ Phase 5: The "Final Exam Simulation" (Days 28‚Äì30)

Focus: Speed, Accuracy, and Question Selection Strategy.

  • Day 28: Solve June 2024 Paper: Strict 3 Hours.
  • Day 29: Solve Dec 2023 Paper: Self-Analysis: Did you get stuck on the "Marginal Costing Case Study"? Did you pick "ABC" first?
  • Day 30: Final Review: Formula Review & Calculator Tricks.

üèÜ Golden Rules for Exam Hall (Paper #12)

  • 1. MCQ First: Finish 30 marks of MCQs in the first 30-40 mins.
  • 2. Order of Attack (Recommended):
    1st: Q2 (Theory + ABC) - Confidence Booster.
    2nd: Q8 (Decision Theory + Resp. Acc) - Short & Sweet.
    3rd: Q7 (Divisional Performance) - High Accuracy.
    4th: Q6 (Standard + Budget) - Calculative.
  • 3. Avoid: Q3 (Marginal Costing Case Study) unless you are 100% sure. It is often a time trap!
