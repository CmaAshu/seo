Experts agree: OM Practical is your "Cash Cow." Secure these 35-40 marks first because they are mathematical and accurate. Strategy: Do NOT solve every question in writing. Use the "Audit Technique" (Check Question ‚Üí Calculate on Calc ‚Üí Check Solution). Only write 1 question per chapter to keep your hand moving.

  • Day 1-2: High-Value Basics: Project Management (PERT/CPM): Focus on "3 Time Estimates" and Critical Path. Machines & Capacity Planning: These are guaranteed questions.
  • Day 3-4: Optimization Kings: Transportation: Master the VAM Method (High probability). Assignment: Secure the easy marks here.
  • Day 5-6: System Analysis: Simulation: Standard 7-mark question. Practice ranges carefully. Queuing Models & Linear Programming: Stick to basic formulations.
  • Day 7-8: Production & Maintenance: Maintenance: Breakdown vs. Preventive Policy (Calculations are key). Production Planning & Control: EOQ/EBQ (just 2-3 sums) and basic flow.
  • Day 9-10: Forecasting & Analytics: Demand Forecasting: Regression method and Moving Averages. Time Study & Work Study: Refresh the formulas.
  • Day 11-12: The Workbook Drill: Crucial Tip: Solve the Institute Workbook questions now. If the paper comes "Standard/Tough," questions will come from here (e.g., Control Charts).

Don't treat SM as "just theory." It is a 40-mark bank. Target scoring 35/40 here. Strategy: Memorize "Head Points" from summary notes. Read the internal content only once to understand.

  • Day 13-14: The Foundation: SM Introduction (Ch 1) & Strategy Alignment (Ch 4): Study these together. They are often linked.
  • Day 15-17: The Core: Strategic Analysis & Planning (Ch 2): SWOT, PESTLE, Portfolio Analysis (BCG/GE). This is the heavyweight chapter.
  • Day 18-19: Execution: Formulation & Implementation (Ch 3): Focus on various levels of strategy.
  • Day 20: Marathon Day: Consolidate your learning by watching a full Revision Marathon. Recommendation: Search for latest exam marathons by top faculties. Simplified explanations of head points are gold.

üèÜ 5 Golden Rules for the Exam Hall

  • 1. Selection is Key: You have to attempt 5 out of 7 descriptive questions. Ideal Mix: 3 OM Practical Questions + 2 SM Questions. (Avoid OM Theory questions if your practical is strong!).
  • 2. MCQ First: Finish the 30 marks MCQ in the first 20-25 minutes.
  • 3. Step Marking: Even if the final answer in OM Practical is wrong, steps give you marks. Show your working notes!
  • 4. No Blank Sheets: In SM, if you forget the exact point, write relevant content around the keyword.
  • 5. Workbooks Matter: Don't ignore the difficult questions in the workbook; they are often the "Googlies" in the exam.
